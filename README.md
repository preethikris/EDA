#### Problem Statement:
Modern supply chains consist of multiple interconnected processes such as manufacturing, inventory management, logistics operations, and demand fulfillment. Organizations often struggle with challenges including stock shortages, logistics delays, cost inefficiencies, route optimization issues, and quality defects. Without integrated analysis across these stages, identifying operational bottlenecks and improving efficiency becomes difficult. Therefore, there is a need for a comprehensive analytical approach to understand relationships between supply chain components and support data-driven decision-making.

#### Objective:
The objective of this project is to conduct an end-to-end exploratory data analysis on a supply chain dataset to identify demand patterns, inventory risks, manufacturing inefficiencies, logistics delays, cost drivers, and quality issues, enabling data-driven decision-making for supply chain optimization.

#### Approach:
Since the dataset was already cleaned and preprocessed, the project focused directly on analytical exploration and insight generation. A structured exploratory data analysis approach was adopted to investigate statistical patterns, correlations, and relationships among supply chain variables. Visualization techniques and correlation analysis were used to uncover trends, inefficiencies, and optimization opportunities across operational stages.

#### Methodology

The methodology involved descriptive statistical analysis, correlation analysis, and visualization-based exploration. Univariate analysis was conducted to understand distributions of price, shipping time, defect rate, and costs, while bivariate and multivariate analyses explored relationships such as price–demand interaction, availability–sales dependency, shipping time–cost tradeoffs, and manufacturing cost–defect rate influence. Logistics efficiency was evaluated using transportation modes, routes, carriers, and location-based shipping performance, while manufacturing and quality analysis assessed production volume, inspection results, and defect patterns. Route optimization and cost analysis were also performed to detect inefficiencies and improvement opportunities.

#### Solution

The analysis provided an integrated understanding of supply chain dynamics by identifying key demand drivers, inventory risks, manufacturing quality concerns, logistics delays, and cost optimization opportunities. It enabled detection of high-demand SKUs, pricing segmentation, and transportation mode tradeoffs, while highlighting inefficient routes and quality risks. Additionally, trigger-based automation concepts were explored to support real-time monitoring for stockouts, defects, and logistics delays.

#### Business Impact

This project supports improved supply chain decision-making by enabling demand-driven inventory management, logistics optimization, quality monitoring, and cost reduction strategies. The integrated analysis highlights the interconnected nature of supply chain stages, allowing organizations to enhance operational efficiency and profitability through data-driven insights.
